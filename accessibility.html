Web Content Accessibility Guidelines

We followed the guide outlined by the Web Accessibility Initiative The outlines we deem most important for our website, but not exclusively important are:

1.1 – Text Alternatives
Provide text alternatives for any non-text content so that it can be changed into other forms people need, such as large print, braille, speech, symbols or simpler language.
1.3 – Adaptable
Create content that can be presented in different ways (for example simpler layout) without losing information or structure.
1.3.4 Orientation
Content does not restrict its view and operation to a single display orientation, such as portrait or landscape, unless a specific display orientation is essential.
1.4 – Distinguishable
Make it easier for users to see and hear content including separating foreground from background.
1.4.1 Use of Color
Color is not used as the only visual means of conveying information, indicating an action, prompting a response, or distinguishing a visual element.
1.4.3 Contrast
The visual presentation of text and images of text has a significant contrast ratio
1.4.10 Reflow
Content can be presented without loss of information or functionality, and without requiring scrolling in two dimensions
3.1 – Readable
Make text content readable and understandable.

Retrieved from:

Web Accessibility Initiative (2024). How to Meet WCAG (Quick Reference). https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/quickref/

GitHub tilgjengelighet

På github siden har vi hatt stort fokus på tilhjenglighet, uansett hva slags enhet som blir brukt. Vi har hger laget en minimalistisk med god lesbarhet. Derfor er det stor og tydelige overskrifter, med gode mellomrom i teksten og et resposivt design for å forsikre at innholdet skal være klart og tydelig uansett om nettsiden brukes på desktop eller mobil.
